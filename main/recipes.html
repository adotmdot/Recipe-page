Baked Lemon Pepper Whole Chicken

Ingredients

  • 1 Whole Chicken
  • 1 1/2 Tablespoons Lemon Pepper Seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon Adobo seasoning
  • 1 tablespoon Citrus Blend Seasoning
  • 1/2 Tablespoon Garlic powder
  • 1/2 Tablespoon Onion powder
  • 1/2 Tablespoon Parsley
  • 1/2 teaspoon Thyme
  • 1 teaspoon Salt
  • 1 teaspoon Black Pepper
  • 1 tablespoon Olive Oil
  • 1 tablespoon Butter

Directions:

Clean and spatchcock the chicken. Pour the olive oil all over the chicken. Mix all seasoning spices in a bowl. Apply seasoning mixture all over the chicken (both sides). Pour melted butter over the chicken. Add a few lemon slices on top. Bake at 340°F for 1 hour, basting the chicken throughout.

Corn Beef with Cauliflower Rice, Boiled Eggs and Salad

Ingredients

  • 1 lb Corned Beef Brisket
  • 4 cups Water
  • 1 Bay Leaf
  • 10 whole Black Peppercorns
  • 1 small Head Cauliflower (riced)
  • 2 large Eggs (hard‐boiled)
  • 2 cups Mixed Salad Greens (washed)
  • ½ cup Cherry Tomatoes (halved)
  • ¼ Cucumber (sliced)
  • 2 tbsp Olive Oil (for cauliflower rice)
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Your favorite vinaigrette or lemon‐olive oil dressing

Directions:

In a large pot, combine the corned beef brisket, water, bay leaf, and peppercorns. Bring to a gentle simmer, then reduce heat, cover, and cook for 2–2½ hours, or until the meat is fork‐tender.
Remove the corned beef from the pot and let it rest for 5–10 minutes before slicing against the grain into ½″‐thick slices.
In a separate pan, heat 2 tbsp olive oil over medium heat. Add riced cauliflower, season with salt & pepper, and sauté for 4–5 minutes until slightly tender. Set aside.

Pot Roast

Ingredients

  • 3–4 lb Beef Chuck Roast
  • 3 Carrots
  • 1 large Yellow Onion
  • 1 tablespoon Olive Oil
  • Salt & Pepper

Directions:

Preheat oven (or slow‐cooker) to 325°F. Pat the chuck roast dry with paper towels, then season generously on all sides with salt and black pepper.
In a large Dutch oven (oven‐safe pot), heat 2 tbsp olive oil over medium‐high. Sear the roast on all sides until well browned (about 4–5 minutes per side). Remove roast and set aside.
In the same pot, add the onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté until the onion is translucent (2–3 minutes). Add the minced garlic and cook another minute.
Stir in 2 tbsp tomato paste; cook for 1 minute to caramelize. Pour in the red wine (if using) and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Let wine reduce by half (about 2–3 minutes).
